# Product

## Register

product

## Users

ROOG is for synth-curious musicians, modular synth users, and browser-audio tinkerers who want to patch a compact eurorack without installing software or owning hardware. They are usually exploring sound, learning signal flow, or testing a patch idea in a visual instrument.

## Product Purpose

ROOG presents a browser-based eurorack with modules, typed patch points, movable panels, and Web Audio routing. Success means a user can understand the rack at a glance, patch audio and CV confidently, and feel like they are touching a loud physical instrument rather than a generic demo UI.

## Brand Personality

Physical, unruly, precise. The interface should combine Moog-style instrument legibility with the abrasive confidence of Death By Audio pedals: tactile controls, high-contrast labels, saturated signal accents, and a little danger around the edges.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, flat gray component libraries, glossy skeuomorphic chrome, decorative glass effects, and sterile music-production software that hides the patching workflow behind menus.

## Design Principles

Make patching readable before it is beautiful.
Keep controls tactile and close to their labels.
Use loud color only where it carries signal, state, or physical energy.
Treat empty HP, rails, screws, labels, and power as part of the instrument.
Prefer direct manipulation over configuration panels.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive states. Preserve keyboard access for jacks and module rearrangement, visible focus states, and reduced-motion alternatives for state transitions. Do not rely on color alone to communicate signal type or compatibility.
